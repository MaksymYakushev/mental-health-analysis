# Mental Health Dashboard

## **Project overview**

This Tableau dashboard provides an interactive analysis of mental health patterns in a remote work environment, offering insights into stress levels, burnout, productivity, and work-life balance across different demographics and regions.

## 🏢 **Business Context**

Companies and organizations increasingly monitor employee wellbeing to understand the impact of remote work on mental health and productivity. This project visualizes these patterns, allowing stakeholders to explore:

- How stress, burnout, and work-life balance vary across employees
- Whether remote, hybrid, or onsite work affects mental wellbeing
- How factors like workload, sleep, and physical activity relate to mental health
- Differences across ages and demographic groups

## 💼 Business Value

This dashboard provides actionable insights that help organizations better understand and manage employee wellbeing in remote and hybrid work environments.

It enables HR teams and management to:

- **Identify critical risk areas:** Quickly detect high levels of stress, burnout, and lack of mental health support across the organization.

- **Improve employee performance:** Understand how mental health factors impact productivity and use this information to design more effective workplace strategies.

- **Optimize workplace policies:** Evaluate how factors such as work format, physical activity, and work-life balance influence employee well-being.

- **Reduce long-term business risks:** Address mental health challenges early to prevent productivity loss, disengagement, and employee turnover.

Overall, the dashboard transforms raw data into meaningful insights, helping organizations move from reactive problem-solving to proactive workforce management.

## 📈 **Dashboard Features**
**<ins>Interactive Filters</ins>** – Users can filter all dashboard data by age group, work format, department, and region for more detailed analysis.  
**<ins>Summary KPI</ins>s** – Displays key metrics such as total number of employees, average weekly working hours, sleep quality, and the share of employees without access to mental health resources.  
**<ins>Demographics</ins>** – A set of visualizations (line, donut, and bar charts) showing the distribution of employees by age, gender, and geographic region.  
**<ins>Years of Work</ins>** – A heatmap matrix that tracks employee tenure across different groups, with a focus on those with 10+ years of experience.  
**<ins>Working Conditions & Productivity</ins>** – Bar charts illustrating overtime levels and percentage changes in employee productivity.  
**<ins>Mental Health Conditions</ins>** – Horizontal charts displaying the prevalence of key conditions such as burnout, depression, and anxiety.  
**<ins>Stress Level Analysis</ins>** – Detailed tables and pie charts analyzing stress levels based on employment type (part-time/full-time) and access to mental health support.  
**<ins>Wellbeing Metrics</ins>** – Visualizations of physical activity frequency, as well as ratings of social isolation and work-life balance.
